Glossary

This page lists the abbreviations that are commonly used in our patterns. These terms help provide a basic understanding of crocheting terms!

Crochet Abbreviations


  • Amigurumi: The art of crocheting small stuffed creatures or objects, often using single crochet stitches in continuous rounds.
  • Blocking: The process of shaping and setting the final dimensions of a crochet project using water and pins or other tools.
  • Blocking Mat: A surface on which crocheted pieces are pinned and blocked to shape and size.
  • Chain (ch): The foundation of most crochet projects, created by pulling a loop through a previous loop, forming a chain-like series of loops.
  • Cluster: A group of stitches worked into the same stitch or space, typically creating a textured or decorative element.
  • Color Change: Transitioning from one yarn color to another within a project.
  • Double Crochet (dc): A taller crochet stitch, often used in many crochet patterns.
  • Foundation Chain: The initial row of chains used to start a crochet project.
  • Frogging: The act of unraveling or ripping out stitches in a project to correct mistakes.
  • Gauge: The number of stitches and rows per inch or centimeter in a crochet project, which determines the size and fit of the finished item.
  • Invisible Join: A technique used to seamlessly finish off a round, making the join virtually invisible.
  • Magic Ring (or Magic Circle): A method for starting projects worked in the round, often used in amigurumi.
  • Pattern: A set of instructions for creating a specific crochet project, including stitch sequences and shaping details.
  • Repeat: Instructions in a pattern that are to be repeated a specified number of times.
  • Round: A circular row of stitches, often used in projects like hats and amigurumi.
  • Row A horizontal line of stitches worked from left to right (for right-handed crocheters).
  • Single Crochet (sc): A basic crochet stitch that creates a tight, dense fabric.
  • Slip Stitch (sl st): A simple stitch used to join rounds or rows and create a smooth edge.
  • Stitch (st): A single unit of crochet, which can vary in height and appearance depending on the stitch used.
  • Tension: The tightness or looseness with which a crocheter holds the yarn while working, which affects the size and appearance of stitches.
  • Treble Crochet (tr): A very tall crochet stitch, commonly used in lacy and openwork projects.
  • Turning Chain: A chain at the beginning of a row or round, which typically serves as the height of the next stitch.
  • WIP: Abbreviation for "Work in Progress," referring to an unfinished crochet project.
  • Yarn Over (yo): Wrapping the yarn around the hook before making a stitch, which creates new loops on the hook.
  • Yarn Weight: A classification system for yarn thickness, which affects the drape and warmth of the finished project (e.g., fingering, bulky, worsted).
